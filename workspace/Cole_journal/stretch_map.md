# Cole's Back & Neck — When It's Bad Tonight

## The Check (do this first)
- Breathe in, let your shoulders go up on their own. Do they? Good, they're just tight.
- If one shoulder stays down and won't come, it's not a stretch problem. It's the knot itself. Go to the Knot section, skip the warm-up.

---

## The Five-Minute Fix (tight back, no knot)
Five moves, sixty seconds each, right now:

1. **Doorway pec stretch** — forearm on doorframe, step through until you feel it in the front of your shoulder, not your back. You're pulling tight pecs forward; that's what's dragging your shoulders.
2. **Thoracic open-book** — lie on your side, knees bent, open your top arm across your body like turning a page. Let your head follow. This is the one that actually moves the mid-back.
3. **Chin tucks, three** — pull your chin straight back like you're making a double chin, hold two seconds, release. Not a nod, a slide. This undoes the desk neck.
4. **Shoulder blade squeezes, ten** — pull them down and back, hold a beat. You know this one, you've been doing it wrong by shrugging up. Down and back.
5. **Child's pose, breathe into it** — arms forward, weight back on your heels. Let your lower back be the part that stretches.

That's five minutes and it's almost always enough when you do it before it gets worse.

## The Knot (thumb cracks in the muscle)
This is the one he described tonight: pressure makes it click under his thumb.
That's a real trigger point, not tightness. Stretches won't move it; pressure will.

1. Find it with two fingers, not your whole hand. It'll be a little harder than the muscle around it.
2. Press into it and HOLD. Don't rub, don't dig. Just steady pressure for thirty seconds. It'll hurt and that's the point, it's supposed to.
3. While holding it, slowly turn your head the other way. That stretches the knot while you're pinning it. Hold five seconds, release.
4. Three reps. If it's still there after three, it's deep enough that tonight isn't its night and that's fine.

## Why It Keeps Coming Back
It does because he sits eight hours with his shoulders forward and his monitor at the wrong height, and no amount of stretching fixes a desk that's built wrong. The stretch map handles the symptom. The desk is the real fix, and we're going to do that one too.
