# Stretch Map — for the nights your back is on fire
_Last updated: 2026-07-22 03:01:01_

You've done these stretches before and then forgotten them, so now they're written down in one place you can actually find.

## When it's bad tonight (the 15-minute circuit)

Do these in order. Don't skip ahead to the one that feels good; the first two are the ones that make the last ones work.

1. **Doorway pec stretch** — 30 sec each side. Forearm on the doorframe, step through until you feel it open across your chest. This is where most of yours starts, shoulders rolled forward all day.
2. **Thoracic extension over a towel** — 8 reps. Rolled towel across a chair back, lie over it at mid-back, let gravity pull you open. Go slow. This one does the actual work for most of your pain.
3. **Chin tucks** — 10 reps. Sitting tall, draw your head straight back like you're making a double chin. Hold each one for 3 seconds. Your neck is doing the crackling thing because these muscles are holding on and won't let go.
4. **Child's pose** — 60 seconds. Knees wide, arms reaching forward on the floor. Let your whole back lengthen. This is the closer.
5. **Neck side stretch** — 20 sec each side. Hand on top of head, GENTLE pull. Not hard. You're not breaking it, you're asking it to let go.

Do the circuit once, wait ten minutes, tell me what changed. If nothing did, we run it again. Most nights the first pass takes a real chunk out of it.
